Who I Am

The Person

Origin

Born into expectation

I was born the eldest son of two eldest children — the firstborn grandchild on both sides. Before I could walk, the room I entered already had a role written for me. Responsible. Dependable. The foundation. I did not choose that. It was handed to me. And somewhere along the way, the expectation became the identity. The weight became familiar. It still is.

What drives me

The unshakeable pillar

My deepest fear is not professional failure — it is failing the people who depend on me. I want to be the kind of person who does not crumble when everything around them shakes. I want to clear the debts my father carried. I want nothing to pass to my children. Everything needs to end with me.

A good day

Calm. Present. Carefree.

A genuinely good day is not a productive one. It is calm and peaceful — surrounded by the people I love, carefree about everything else. No agenda. No performance. Just being present in the room with the people who matter. That is the whole aspiration, honestly.

The mirror moment

My wife's resume

She looked at the polished, keyword-optimised resume I built for her and said it did not feel like her. She felt less confident with it than with something simpler and more honest. That hit me harder than I expected. Because I realised I had been doing the same thing to myself. That moment started everything I am trying to build differently now.

"Life is much simpler than we see. Expectations keep it disoriented. A peace of mind is the key to a healthier life. Help everyone around you — without any expectations in return."

— What I genuinely believe

My Story

The Journey

1991 — Kerala, India

Born into a family of firsts

Born on 15 March 1991 — the firstborn of two eldest children, the firstborn grandchild on both sides. Grew up in a household shaped by public service, quiet discipline, and the kind of responsibility that does not announce itself but is simply always there.

2009–2013 — Karnataka, India

The engineering degree that was not my choice

My family chose Mechanical Engineering at VTU. I wanted computers, teaching, management — something with people and ideas. I completed the degree with first class anyway. It taught me that you can excel at things that are not yours and still find your way to what is.

2014–2016 — Mumbai & India

Finding the foundation

Procurement Coordinator at Gtech Equipments — a learning platform. Took an MBA in Supply Chain Management and discovered that management was the language I had been looking for all along. Operations, logistics, leadership, strategy — finally, the right room.

2016–2017 — Kerala, India

Three revenue streams and total ownership

Business Development Manager at Embsoft Technologies — a startup where I ran three commercial streams simultaneously with no support structure and complete accountability. 75% revenue growth in ten months. Learned that I thrive when ownership is total.

2017–2021 — Kerala, India

The blank page that became a company

Two people. A mandate from the MD and Chairman. Build a Medical Device and Trading Division from nothing. Four years later: USD 1.4M in annual revenue, 23 brands, 300+ institutional accounts, 80+ employees, an e-commerce platform, an acquired manufacturer, and a registered private limited entity. Three promotions in three years. The proudest chapter — not for what it achieved, but for what it proved.

2021–2025 — Toronto, Canada

The family chapter

Left India in October 2021 for Canada — seeking better balance and a fresh chapter. Completed two postgraduate certifications. Worked at Tim Hortons where I changed how people saw work without saying a word — earned respect through action alone. Promoted within 12 months. After five years, chose family over career and came home. Not because Canada defeated me. Because some things matter more.

2026 — Dubai, UAE

The fresh start

Relocated to Dubai in May 2026 with my wife. A new city, a new chapter, the same person. Building visibility in a new market. Looking for the right opportunity — not just any opportunity. Still believes the best things are built slowly, honestly, and from the ground up.

How I Think

The Thinker

Systems thinking

Everything is a system — business, people, processes, relationships. When I understand the core foundation of something I can navigate it faster than most. I don't memorise. I connect. New industry, new context, same underlying mechanics.

AI & the future of work

Deeply interested in how artificial intelligence is reshaping how humans work, create, and find meaning. Not from fear — from genuine curiosity about what remains irreplaceable and what the transition demands of us.

The unseen capable

Most hiring systems filter for credentials, not capability. The people who built things no certificate validates — they are everywhere. Recognising them and helping them articulate what they have actually done is something I care about.

Authenticity over performance

Polished and honest are not the same thing. The world is full of people performing confidence, performing success, performing wellness. I believe in showing up as you actually are, and letting that be enough.

Harmony, not as a word

I want a world where people see each other as people — not through the lens of race, nation, sexuality, or agenda. Today's world runs on anxiety. There is still enough goodwill left to build something better.

Building, not managing

Managers run what exists. Builders create what doesn't. The distinction matters to me deeply. I have spent my career in situations where the thing I was asked to manage did not yet exist. That is where I am most myself.

How I Show Up

The Social

I help because I want to help. Not a specific group, not a target audience — anyone who needs to be heard.

I believe the most undervalued thing one person can do for another is simply listen without agenda. Acknowledge their existence. Acknowledge their struggle. Not to fix it immediately — but to clear the noise so they can think again.

The room in someone's mind that gets filled by anxiety, by comparison, by the weight of others' expectations — when someone genuinely hears you, that room gets a little lighter. And lighter rooms make space for new things.

I am starting to share more publicly — not to build an audience, but because if something I have lived through can help one person feel less alone, that is enough reason.

  • Harmony — I see people as people. Race, nation, sexuality, background — none of it changes how I engage with someone.
  • Honest help — I give people what they expected, what others expected, and sometimes what nobody expected. Multiple perspectives on the same problem. That is how I think.
  • Quiet influence — I earn respect through action, not volume. I changed how people saw work at Tim Hortons without a single word about it. That is the only kind of influence I want.
  • No agenda — I help without expecting return. That is how goodwill actually moves in the world.
  • Hopeful realism — Angry about a world running on anxiety. Hopeful because enough people still want good for each other. Both things are true at once.
